Gordon Murray

A Tribute to the visionary creator of the iconic McLaren F1

  • 1946 - Born on June 18 in Durban, South Africa.
  • 1967 - Graduated from Natal Technical College with a degree in mechanical engineering.
  • 1969 - Joined the Brabham Formula One team as a designer and engineer.
  • 1973 - Designed the Brabham BT42, his first Formula One car.
  • 1978 - Developed the Brabham BT46B, known as the "fan car," which used a fan to generate downforce.
  • 1981 - Appointed Technical Director of the Brabham team.
  • 1983 - Brabham BT52, designed by Murray, won the Formula One World Championship with Nelson Piquet as the driver.
  • 1987 - Left Brabham and founded "McLaren Cars Limited" to design the McLaren F1.
  • 1992 - The McLaren F1 was unveiled to the public. It featured a central driving position, a gold-lined engine bay, and a naturally aspirated BMW V12 engine. The F1 became the world's fastest production car with a top speed of 240.1 mph.
  • 1994 - The McLaren F1 GTR, a racing version of the F1, debuted at the 24 Hours of Le Mans and won the race outright.
  • 1997 - McLaren F1 production officially concluded after 106 units were built, including road and race versions.
  • 2004 - Established "Gordon Murray Design," a company focused on developing innovative and environmentally friendly vehicles.
  • 2010 - The T.25 city car concept was introduced, showcasing Murray's approach to lightweight and efficient vehicle design.
  • 2017 - Revealed the "iStream" manufacturing process, a lightweight and cost-effective method for producing vehicles.
  • 2019 - Announced the development of the Gordon Murray Automotive T.50, a spiritual successor to the McLaren F1, promising a driver-centric design and advanced aerodynamics.
  • 2020 - The T.50 was officially unveiled, featuring a naturally aspirated V12 engine, a central driving position, and a ground-effect aerodynamics system.
